Agile Toolkit contains everything you need for your web development. It does not rely on any other libraries or frameworks. Learning to develop with Agile Toolkit is exciting and interesting process.

It's easy to start using Agile Toolkit. However, to become a power user it will take you days or even weeks. Your best guide is Learning "Agile Toolkit" book. You can always find it on this site under Documentation / Learning section.

Find a skilled developer for your web application. See list of vendors who offer project, addon or theme development for Agile Toolkit.

Other ways to learn

If you preferr to watch and listen, you can look through few of our fast-paced screencasts (they are not using most up-to-date version of Agile Toolkit). You will find out how to do simple tasks or create your new application completely from scratch.

For some "example" applications you should take a look at Invite-me Project. It migth come handy next time Google might be offering invite-only service for requesting and sharing invites. There is also Code-pad if you want to show and share your code.

There are full high-scale applications available under Open-Source license such as Colubris Project. This project implements advanced time-management system. This website is also written in Agile Toolkit. It is more focused on presentation of information rather than interraction.

We are going to add more tutorials soon into our Tutorial section soon, those who know Symfony can get good idea about how Agile Toolkit compares by reading through Agile Jobeet tutorial.

The Community

Our focus was to finish documentation and API before we start working on community. We do realise that community pages are very important and comments and discussion boards are almost here.

Meanwhile, register on StackOverflow.com and use "atk4" tag to ask your question related to Agile Toolkit. Using search is also quite useful for finding what you need. If you can't found something, please use contact form and we will help you over email (for free).

You can also fork Agile Toolkit on GitHub or post problems on our Issue Tracker.

Staying up-to-date

There are many things coming to Agile Toolkit, it's extensions and the documentation website. You can follow the upates by looking up on our What's new? page or reading through Agile Toolkit Blog.
